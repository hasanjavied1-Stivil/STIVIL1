Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a stormwater report for my DA in Sydney's Eastern Suburbs?

Yes — most development applications in the Eastern Suburbs (including Randwick, Waverley, and Woollahra council areas) require a stormwater management plan and, for larger sites, an on-site detention (OSD) design. STIVIL can prepare all required stormwater documentation tailored to your council's specific requirements.

When is a flood assessment required for a Sydney development?

A flood impact assessment or flood report is commonly required when a site is identified as flood-affected, or near an overland flow path. City of Sydney, Randwick, and other Eastern Suburbs councils regularly request TUFLOW modelling reports as part of the DA process. Our team can advise from the pre-DA stage.

What is an OSD system and does my development need one?

An on-site detention (OSD) system manages stormwater runoff from new developments to ensure flows don't exceed pre-development levels. Most medium-to-large developments in the City of Sydney and Eastern Suburbs LGAs require an OSD system. STIVIL handles analysis, design, and council compliance documentation.

Can STIVIL handle both civil and stormwater design in one engagement?

Yes — this is one of STIVIL's key strengths. Our integrated approach means civil grading, drainage, and stormwater designs are coordinated from the outset, reducing RFIs, clashes, and council queries. We regularly act as the lead civil and stormwater consultant for residential and commercial projects across Sydney.
